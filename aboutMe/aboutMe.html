About Me

I am currently in my final year of Bachelor's in Computer Science. As a university student, my goals have been to take community initiatives while working a full-time job and growing my network. I am always eager to learn and develop my skills and personality. I also love cats and gardening. Excited to see where this journey takes me!

Some of my most cherished Community Projects

Self Recognition through Art

Tirana Albania

25 May 2024

Me and my brother organized a fun activity with high school and university students which aimed for self discovery and goal setting through creative art. The participants had the opportunity to express ourselves through painting, coloring, making vision boards, and music. Together we discussed and shared our experiences and had a session on how to create SMART Goals.

TOTe-ALLY Plastic Free

Permet Albania

6-8 July 2022

Fun Meeting with children who participated in a summer camp to discuss the importance of protecting the environment, and educating their families on plastic pollution. Following the activity, collaborating with a local tailor company and the municipality, we distributed tote bags around the community, making a bigger impact on reducing plastic waste.

Gender Equality Workshop

Permet, Kelcyre Albania

August 2021

A key issue I care about is gender equality. In June 2019, I took the first step of dealing with this problem by spreading awareness through a Gender Equality Workshop I organized, sharing everything that I had learned on The IDEAL 2021 Workshop. Two years later, after gaining more resources and funding from the BridgingBackground Program, I collaborated with a student from my hometown organizing a Girl Empowerment Workshop in two different towns in order to have a bigger impact in the community. I am particularly proud of this initiative, as it allowed me to empower girls by sharing success stories of women from our own community, inspiring them to pursue their own goals.

Fact-Checking Online Workshop

online

12 December 2020

I teamed up with a student from Albania and another from Mali to organize an online fact-checking workshop. The program attracted 35 participants from countries including Albania, Mali, Israel, Palestine, and Turkey. We led participants through a general overview of news literacy followed by an exploration of information neighborhoods, which include news, opinion, propaganda, and disinformation, and how to distinguish between them.

These activities were funded by American Councils.
